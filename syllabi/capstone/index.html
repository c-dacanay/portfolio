Course Info

Description

The Capstone Studio course asks students to produce an interactive project with documentation, a research paper, and a personal portfolio.

The interactive project will illustrate students‚Äô unique interests as well as evidence of competency within the field of interactive media production. Students are encouraged to develop their project around a theme previously explored in their work. Projects will be presented and critiqued repeatedly throughout the capstone process to peers, faculty, and industry professionals. A final presentation of the interactive project will be delivered late in the semester.

The research paper (4000‚Äì5000 words) will focus on at least one aspect of the interactive project: e.g. culture, theory, philosophy, or history, the project context, and/or production methods. For example, students may write about their project‚Äôs reception by a set of specific users, or by users who are part of a larger culture, society, or market. It is important that students think beyond the project itself and situate it in a broader context accessible through research. The research paper will include an annotated bibliography of the books and other resources they used for their research.

Students will also be guided in the production of an online portfolio to showcase their work and accomplishments to the outside world. Graduates will be evaluated by their portfolio when applying for jobs, graduate school, artist residencies, grants, and the like. Portfolios will be tailored to the demands of each student‚Äôs future goals and target audience.

Objectives

  1. Understand and be able to conceptualize, prototype and implement a complete interactive media project.
  2. Develop an understanding of contemporary professional, academic and artistic applications of media arts, and the place of their own work in those environments.
  3. Understand the importance of research and scholarship as a part of the project development process.
  4. Become comfortable with critique and giving feedback to peers.
  5. Communicate their ideas effectively both in oral and written presentations.

üìù Research Paper and Peer Review

Research papers will be required to roughly follow the organizational conventions and format of submissions to well-known media arts conferences, such as ISEA. The class is designed to help students write this research paper incrementally, section by section (Abstract, Introduction, Related work, Methodology, Conclusion, etc.), alongside production work on their interactive project. In Week 7, students will submit an initial version of this paper as a ‚Äúproposal‚Äù for peer review from their fellow students (and other stakeholders, such as faculty members and field experts), following the conventions of peer-reviewed conferences. (Structure and format of this paper may be adjusted on an individual basis, according to student-identified needs and outcomes.)

üì•Assignment Submission

It is expected that you will spend 6 to 8 hours a week on the class outside of class itself. This will include reviewing material, reading, completing assignments and so on. Please budget your time accordingly.

Most weeks have associated assignments. Please refer to the schedule for details on assignment requirements. In general, the assignment for each week relates to the topics of in-class lectures and tutorials. Assignments are designed not just as a way for students to apply what they learned in class, but also to meaningfully advance the student work on their research papers and interactive projects. (Starting in Week 6, students are also expected to make weekly progress on the production of their interactive project, in addition to the required assignments.)

Each assignment will be marked as complete (full credit), partially complete (half credit), or incomplete (no credit). To be complete an assignment should meet the criteria specified in the syllabus including documentation. If significant portions are simply not attempted or the assignment is turned in late (up to 1 week) then it may be marked partially complete. If an assignment is late, or shows no attempt to meet the criteria specified, it will be marked incomplete.
