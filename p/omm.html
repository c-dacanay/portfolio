One More Multiverse

overview

One More Multiverse (OMM) was a highly ambitious platform for telling cinematic stories with tabletop games. I began as the sole UX designer and was later promoted to Design Lead, where I lead every part of the design process in close collaboration with leadership, product, and growth teams. As we grew I initiated and trained our five person playtesting team that delivered player insights to the product team at a regular cadence.

project: redesigning the HUD

As OMM planned to launch the alpha product out of closed beta it became essential to reorganize the HUD on our virtual table top. Due to a long phase of engineering led development the platform was bloated with tools that were difficult to discover and use. New users often needed to attend our staff-led tutorial events in order to get started. We wanted to use the opportunity to cut some deprecated features while also organizing the HUD to be able to reasonably scaffold features in the future.

OMMâ€™s core mission was to transform the way game masters and players could tell stories visually in the online space. Therefore the primary challenge became how to balance clarity and flexibility with the huge array of creative tools that platform had to offer. We needed to support the creative needs of our storytellers while still making the tool accessible enough for new players to hop in. In order to build confidence, we needed to carefully observe how our current users played and assess where they struggled. We also used the opportunity to determine if legacy features were being used through data-driven methods.

research

I created a feature map of all the features in the current HUD with their location on the screen, including modals and nav items. Visualizing this map gave us a sense of the overwhelming amount of user choice presented when first opening the platform.

I took a semi-random sampling of 35 features to try and determine different types of categorization. After conducting a card sort we decided to organize features according to what they modified: the Verse (eg. the world settings), the Level (eg. placing assets on the map), or Play (eg. chat, rolling dice).

In the process of developing proposals for new designs I also conducted a series of interviews where I observed players creating new Verses, prep for sessions, and play community games to test conclusions. After playtesting mocks of the designs for a handful of players we began refining and polishing the UI to begin implementation.
